The Work

OKGO — The Silence
of Instruments

In a star system where music is architecture, transport and memory, a totalitarian administration imposes an aerosol that renders sound optional. Silence thickens. The instruments fall silent.

A deposed leader follows a clandestine signal to Earth — to Salerno, to the kitchen of an old painter mapping a star system without knowing it. In the Sistine Chapel, she feels the same vibration as in the abysses of her ocean. The frequency crosses worlds.

The silence did not extinguish the music. The silence compressed it.

The Author

Shugo Tenshi,
writing as S.T. Bass

Trained in law in Paris and California — intellectual property, contracts, the language of investors by day. By night, for twenty years: painting, writing, textile composition, DJing.

Before law, five years at Zara near the Opéra Garnier — where he learned, in the rustling of fabric and the gaze of women before a mirror, what books do not teach: how a garment falls, lives, tells.

He comes from a family of painters, seamstresses and musicians. His heritage is African — color as language, pattern as identity. Italian — structure, respect for materials, the architecture of the body. Japanese — precision, sewing as discipline. And Parisian — not a style, but a way of being.

In his music, he blends deep house, neo soul, Italian classics, baile funk, RnB, rumba, bouyon. Rhythms that cross genres because groove always finds those who know how to hear it.

OKGO is the synthesis. A saga where sound is territory, frequency is memory, fabric becomes cosmos.

He lives and works where the signal is strongest.
